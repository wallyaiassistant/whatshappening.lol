Labour "Respects" Court Order After 70 Years of Paying €400 Rent in Valletta

By Zykelle Galea · 20/02/26, 8:45 PM

The Labour Party has graciously announced that it "respects the Court's decisions, as it always does" — in this case, a court decision ordering the State to pay €254,307 to the owners of a Valletta property that Labour's club has been occupying since 1953 at a rent so low it makes controlled substances look expensive.

How low? In 2014, the Valletta Labour Club was paying €406.40 per year for a property on Republic Street. That's €33.87 per month. In Valletta. The capital city. Triq ir-Repubblika. For less than the cost of a family dinner at a mediocre restaurant, Labour secured twelve months of prime commercial property access. Min jistħi jmut bil-ġuħ — and Labour has never been accused of excessive shame.

The Numbers That Make You Weep

According to Times of Malta, Judge Francesco Depasquale ruled that rent control laws allowed the club to pay a "disproportionately" low rent for over seven decades. By 2024, the rent had climbed to the dizzying heights of €667.16 per year. The club also paid five per cent of its annual income to the owners: €66.87 in 2022, €168.91 in 2023, and €559.79 in 2024.

Let those numbers sink in. A political party — one that has governed Malta for most of the last decade — was paying less in annual commercial rent than most Maltese people pay for their monthly Netflix, Spotify, and gym membership combined.

The Spin Cycle

Now watch the reactions. This is where it gets truly beautiful.

The PN went full attack mode, as reported by the Malta Independent: "Labour grabs, and the people pay." They pointed out that Labour has accumulated €1.6 million in penalties for property abuses in recent months — all paid from taxpayer money. Strong words, delivered with the righteous energy of a party that definitely has no skeletons in its own property closet. Definitely.

Labour's response? A masterclass in political deflection. First, they noted the court said they wouldn't be evicted — because the Valletta PL Local Committee cleverly bought a 4% (1/25th) share of the property back in 2004. You can't evict a co-owner. Chess, not checkers.

Then came the whataboutism: "The Nationalist Party would do better to examine how it concealed its debt in a cosmetic exercise." Translation: "Yes we paid €400 rent for 70 years, but have you seen their accounting?" Il-ħmar qal lil-ħmar: kemm għandek widnejk kbar.

The Portal Showdown

MaltaToday led with the facts — "derisory rent," "tenants escape eviction" — clean and devastating. Times of Malta went detailed on the legal mechanics. Malta Independent ran both the PN attack and Labour's response side by side. Newsbook framed it as Labour "continuing to occupy" the property — subtle, but pointed.

And ONE News? They published Labour's statement in full. The bit about respecting courts. The bit about the PN's finances. Conveniently leaving out the part where a Constitutional Court found that Labour's occupation breached the owners' fundamental human rights. Just... minor details. Merħba.

talk.mt, Labour's other media flank, went with a similar approach: highlight Labour's "reform" of rent laws, minimise the €254K payout, and pivot immediately to attacking the PN's financial management. Because nothing says "we respect the court" like immediately changing the subject.

The owners of the Republic Street property, who have waited 70 years for fair compensation, will reportedly use the €254,307 to buy a property in Valletta — where they can charge actual market rent. To anyone except a political party.
