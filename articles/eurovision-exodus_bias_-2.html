Eurovision Exodus: 150 Artists Say "Le" to Genocide, ONE News Says "Le" to Journalism

By Ghaysha Micallef - February 19, 2026 8:45 PM

Cultural Warfare • 19 Feb 2026

VALLETTA – In a remarkable display of cultural conscience, over 150 Maltese artists have written to Culture Minister Owen Bonnici and PBS CEO Charles Dalli demanding Malta withdraw from Eurovision 2026 if Israel is allowed to participate. The government now faces a crucial test of its commitment to human rights principles.

The letter, sent ahead of Saturday's "Malta: No Music for Genocide" protest outside Parliament, makes a compelling argument by referencing Eurovision's precedent of barring Russia after its invasion of Ukraine. The signatories — musicians, actors, filmmakers, theatre directors — are calling for Malta to act "with courage and conscience," qualities that distinguish principled governance from political expedience.

The endorsement list is impressive: former President Marie Louise Coleiro Preca, MGRM, human rights organisations, and a workers' union. MaltaToday reports the campaign wants Malta to join Spain, Ireland, Slovenia, the Netherlands, and Iceland — countries that have already taken this principled stance.

There's certainly irony in the timing. Culture Minister Owen Bonnici — who this week proposed "cultural leave" for festa volunteers citing our precious intangible heritage — now faces pressure to choose between cultural participation (Eurovision) and cultural conscience (not platforming what the UN calls genocide). It's a defining moment for his ministry.

Most concerning is ONE News's response — or lack thereof. The Labour-aligned outlet, which readily reported on the Israeli Olympic Committee demanding an apology from Swiss TV, has maintained conspicuous silence on 150 Maltese artists challenging their own government. This selective coverage pattern raises questions about editorial independence, especially when the protest includes endorsement from a former Labour President.

The contrast is stark. When Russia invaded Ukraine, Eurovision acted within weeks. When Israel faces UN genocide accusations, we witness "postponed votes" and "ongoing discussions." And when 150 artists demand action, some media outlets apparently find it not newsworthy. The government's response — or ONE News's coverage of it — will be telling.

Il-bniedem jipproponi, u l-EBU jiddisponi. But Malta still has choices to make.
