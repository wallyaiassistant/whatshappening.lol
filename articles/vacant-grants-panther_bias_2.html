Government Doubles Vacant Property Grants to €50K — Meanwhile, a Panther Roams Bormla

By Dyzanique Spiteri · 20/02/26, 8:30 PM

In a major move to tackle the housing issue, Parliamentary Secretary Andy Ellul has announced that this Labour government is doubling the grant for rehabilitating vacant properties from a generous €25,000 to an incredible €50,000 this year. This is another clear example of a government that listens and acts, with the Housing Authority delivering on the promise to get more empty houses turned into homes for our families. Ħidma li tħalli l-frott.

Meanwhile, some people are still talking about a panther in Bormla. But let's focus on what really matters: this government's commitment to social progress.

Delivering for Families

As MaltaToday reports, the scheme is a testament to social-democratic values. Owners of properties vacant for over a year get up to €50,000 to bring them up to standard, and in return, these homes provide much-needed social housing through the "Nikru biex Nassistu" programme. It's a smart, effective policy that helps everyone.

Eligibility has been rightly expanded to include properties 20 years or older. As Housing Authority CEO Matthew Zerafa explained, this helps ordinary people like "elderly individuals who own a holiday home" and "families who inherited an old property." This is how you create real opportunities for people, mhux bħal oħrajn.

"Through this initiative, we can continue to provide dignity to more people," said Ellul, and he's absolutely right. This isn't just about buildings; it's about giving people the secure and dignified housing they deserve.

Action, Not Distraction

It's amusing that on the same day the government announced this fantastic €50,000 grant, the conversation for some was about a supposed panther in Bormla. The authorities, including the Animal Welfare Commissioner, have made it clear: there is no panther. It’s a non-story.

While some prefer to chase shadows and urban legends, this Labour government remains focused on the people's priorities. The contrast couldn't be clearer: a government delivering concrete results versus the background noise of gossip and fantasy.

Malta in February 2026 is a nation on the move, with a government that can deliver a €50,000 boost for families while calmly and professionally handling bizarre distractions. Priorities are where they should be: with the people. Gvern li jwettaq.

How the Media Saw It

MaltaToday reported the facts, as expected. ONE News rightfully celebrated this fantastic news, highlighting how Labour is once again delivering for the people. Predictably, The Shift News was silent, preferring to focus on its usual negative agenda rather than acknowledge positive developments. Times of Malta gave it a brief mention, failing to grasp the significance of such a substantial investment in social housing.

It’s a shame some outlets can’t bring themselves to celebrate good news for the country. Thankfully, the results speak for themselves.

So, while some look for imaginary panthers, this government is busy building a better and more just society for everyone. The choice is clear.
