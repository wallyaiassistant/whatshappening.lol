Occupy Land Illegally for Decades? Here's Your 50-Year Deal, Sir

By Shunique Farrugia - February 19, 2026 10:45 AM

Land of Opportunity • 19 Feb 2026

TA' QALI – In what can only be described as the Maltese Dream™, Parliament's accounts committee has approved a 50-year concession for the Club Numero Uno site at Ta' Qali — rewarding decades of occupation without a valid legal title with the kind of long-term security most homeowners can only fantasise about.

Odel Co. Ltd., the private operator behind the venue, has been using the site since 1976. For most of that time, everything was above board. But there was also the small matter of 862 square metres the company had been occupying "without a formal title." In any other country, this might be called squatting. In Malta, it's called a business opportunity.

The punishment for this decades-long land grab? A one-time penalty of €431,000 — and a shiny new 50-year lease at an annual ground rent of €126,212. The site's freehold value? A cool €6.3 million.

Culture Minister Owen Bonnici presented the resolution, and the Lands Authority CEO Robert Vella helpfully explained that the law on concessions was designed to "avoid lengthy court proceedings when a place has been in use for so long." Translation: if you occupy something long enough, it becomes policy.

This latest episode follows the Ta' Qali gravel saga — the €311,000 "blunder" that critics say was intentionally designed to squeeze money out of a picnic area. Ta' Qali, it seems, is less a national park and more a masterclass in creative land management.

For aspiring entrepreneurs, the lesson is clear: find some government land, set up shop, wait 48 years, and Parliament will eventually formalise your arrangement. Patience is the ultimate business plan.
