By Ghaysha Micallef · 20/02/26, 8:15 PM

Their cynical solution? Deploy the volatile Franco Debono as a human hand grenade to blow up any possibility of substantive debate. X'tistenna minn dawn?

The Calculated Chaos

What followed was textbook Labour manipulation. The show imploded into chaos — exactly as intended. Police were called in — problem solved. Any risk of Farrugia exposing Labour's internal contradictions? Eliminated through orchestrated mayhem.

The particularly cynical aspect here is how Labour managed to make the PN look unstable while simultaneously protecting their own dirty laundry. Classic misdirection: create the crisis, blame the opposition, walk away clean. Min ma jkunx jaf, ma jkunx jista' jitkellem.

The Twelve-Year Aftermath

What makes this story particularly damning is Franco Debono's subsequent twelve-year career of institutional disruption. Labour knew exactly what they were unleashing, yet they did it anyway because protecting the party image mattered more than protecting democratic institutions.

Meanwhile, Simon Busuttil — the only participant who actually came prepared for democratic debate — maintained his composure throughout the engineered chaos. Whatever you think of his politics, his behaviour that night demonstrated the kind of principled leadership Labour apparently feared from their own deputy.
