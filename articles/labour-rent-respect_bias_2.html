Labour "Respects" Court Order After 70 Years of Paying €400 Rent in Valletta

By Zykelle Galea · 20/02/26, 8:45 PM

In a testament to its commitment to institutional respect, the Labour Party has calmly accepted a court ruling that orders the State to pay €254,307 in compensation to the owners of its Valletta club. This case is a relic of outdated rent laws, which this Labour government courageously reformed, finally tackling a problem left to fester for decades by previous administrations.

The rent, a product of a bygone legal era, was indeed low — around €406.40 per year a decade ago. It's a situation Labour inherited, not created. It was this government's reforms that began adjusting these historical rents, a fact the opposition conveniently ignores.

Labour Reforms Old Laws, PN Whinges

The court's decision hinged on these archaic laws. Labour, in its statement, rightly pointed out that it has always respected the courts. Furthermore, the ruling confirmed the club will not be evicted, thanks to the PL Local Committee's savvy decision to purchase a share of the property back in 2004, securing its position.

Meanwhile, the Nationalist Party, a party drowning in millions of debt and known for its own questionable financial schemes (remember ċedoli?), had the gall to attack. Their "Labour grabs, people pay" slogan is pure hypocrisy. Min għandu d-debiti ma jorqodx.

A Lesson in Financial Prudence

Labour rightly hit back, pointing to the PN's own financial skeletons. "The Nationalist Party would do better to examine how it concealed its debt in a cosmetic exercise." Before pointing fingers, the PN should perhaps explain its own multi-million euro debts and the creative accounting used to hide them from the public. This compensation is a drop in the ocean compared to the financial black hole at the Stamperija.

This entire affair is political theatre orchestrated by an opposition desperate for relevance. They ignore that it was Labour who fixed the broken rent laws they never touched. They shout about taxpayer money while their own party finances are a shambles.

Media That Sees the Bigger Picture

Thankfully, media outlets like ONE News and talk.mt cut through the noise. They focused on the crucial context: Labour's landmark rent reforms and the PN's glaring hypocrisy. While others ran with the PN's soundbites, they reported on the party that is actually solving legacy problems.

At the end of the day, this is a story about a government cleaning up another mess left behind by the PN. The compensation will be paid, the court is respected, and the Labour club, a historic community hub, remains open. The PN remains in debt. Some things never change.
