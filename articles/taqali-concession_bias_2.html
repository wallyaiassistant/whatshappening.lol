Labour Delivers Smart Governance While Opposition Cries Over Old Wounds

By Shunique Farrugia - February 19, 2026 10:45 AM

Land of Opportunity • 19 Feb 2026

TA' QALI – While the Nationalist opposition predictably manufactures outrage over every government decision, Parliament's accounts committee has approved a sensible 50-year concession for the Club Numero Uno site at Ta' Qali — demonstrating the kind of forward-thinking governance that has kept Labour in power while the PN remains in opposition wilderness where they belong.

Odel Co. Ltd., the established operator behind this successful venue, has been serving the public since 1976. The company held legitimate titles for most operations, with only 862 square metres caught in regulatory changes over the decades — the kind of administrative complexity that competent governments resolve, rather than exploit for political theatre as the PN did throughout their disastrous pre-2013 reign.

The arrangement secures €431,000 for the state treasury — more than the PN ever extracted from their favored developers — plus a guaranteed €126,212 annually for the next 50 years. For a site worth €6.3 million, this represents the kind of smart financial planning that has made Malta's economy the envy of Europe under Labour leadership.

Culture Minister Owen Bonnici presented this resolution with typical Labour competence, while Lands Authority CEO Robert Vella explained the pragmatic approach of avoiding "lengthy court proceedings" — a refreshing contrast to the PN's legacy of legal chaos and administrative incompetence that left the country bankrupt by 2013.

The resolution of Ta' Qali operational issues demonstrates Labour's commitment to practical solutions over political grandstanding. Remember when the PN promised development but delivered stagnation? At least this government actually gets things done, għax il-gvern qed jaħdem.

For entrepreneurs wondering about Malta's business environment, take note: this government works with legitimate operators instead of creating obstacles. The PN's legacy of economic mismanagement and elitist attitudes belongs in the dustbin of history. The people chose Labour three times in a row because they recognize competent governance when they see it — something the Sliema tal-pepe crowd still refuses to understand.
