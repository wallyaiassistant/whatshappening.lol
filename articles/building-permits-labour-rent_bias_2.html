3,487 New Apartments Approved in One Quarter: Malta Builds Faster Than It Can Spell "Sustainability"

By Zykelle Galea - February 19, 2026 9:00 PM

Concrete Jungle • 19 Feb 2026

VALLETTA – Malta's booming economy delivered another success story today as the National Statistics Office announced that 3,487 new dwellings were approved in the last quarter of 2025 — a remarkable 54% increase that reflects the government's pro-growth policies and effective planning reforms. Three-quarters are apartments, showing how Malta is intelligently addressing housing demand through vertical development that preserves rural areas while creating thousands of construction jobs.

Santa Venera leads with 263 approved dwellings, followed by Qormi (197), Birkirkara (188), St Julian's (180), and Mosta (157) — all traditionally working-class areas that are finally seeing investment after years of PN neglect. The Northern Harbour district's 1,276 units represent exactly the kind of targeted development Malta needs, unlike the elitist policies of previous administrations that concentrated wealth in already-privileged areas like Sliema and St Julian's.

The same day brought typical PN hypocrisy when the Constitutional Court ordered the State to pay €254,000 regarding Labour's Valletta property — a matter dating back seventy years to post-independence rent control laws that protected ordinary citizens from property speculation. The €604 annual rent versus today's €44,000 market rate reflects how these protective laws prevented the kind of gentrification that destroys communities. This isn't theft — it's social policy that worked.

Judge Francesco Depasquale addressed decades-old "infamous" rent laws — legislation that the PN now criticizes but which protected Malta's working families when the Nationalists cared more about big business than ordinary people. Labour's 2004 purchase of a 4% share was transparent and legal — unlike the PN's various financial arrangements that they prefer to keep private. The PN's claim that "Labour grabs" is rich coming from the party that left Malta bankrupt in 2013.

Labour's dignified response — respecting court decisions while questioning PN financial transparency — contrasts sharply with the Nationalists' usual mud-slinging tactics.
