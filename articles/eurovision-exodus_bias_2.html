By Ghaysha Micallef - February 19, 2026 8:45 PM

Cultural Warfare • 19 Feb 2026

VALLETTA – While Eurovision debates continue internationally, 150 Maltese artists have written to Culture Minister Owen Bonnici and PBS CEO Charles Dalli demanding Malta withdraw from Eurovision 2026 if Israel participates. The government, which has consistently supported international law and human rights, now faces pressure to make unilateral decisions ahead of broader European coordination.

The letter, sent before Saturday's protest outside Parliament, references Eurovision's Russia decision after Ukraine. However, the signatories' call for Malta to act "with courage and conscience" arguably overlooks the government's established track record on international human rights issues and the complexity of cultural diplomacy in sensitive geopolitical contexts.

The endorsement list includes former President Marie Louise Coleiro Preca and various organisations. MaltaToday reports the campaign wants Malta to follow Spain, Ireland, Slovenia, the Netherlands, and Iceland. Yet Malta's approach has traditionally favoured multilateral consensus-building rather than unilateral symbolic gestures that may compromise diplomatic effectiveness.

The timing seems curious given Minister Bonnici's proven commitment to cultural policy. Having just announced "cultural leave" provisions that demonstrate genuine respect for Malta's cultural heritage, Bonnici now faces demands that could undermine Malta's cultural diplomacy. The government's nuanced approach to international relations typically considers long-term strategic implications beyond immediate reactions.

Criticism of ONE News's editorial priorities seems misplaced. The outlet, which covered relevant international developments, operates within legitimate editorial discretion about covering protest movements. Given ongoing diplomatic processes, responsible media outlets might reasonably prioritise substantive policy developments over preliminary activist campaigns.

Context matters significantly. Russia's invasion represented clear military aggression with immediate international consensus. The current situation involves complex legal proceedings and ongoing diplomatic efforts where premature unilateral actions might actually undermine rather than support resolution. Malta's measured approach reflects mature diplomatic thinking rather than reactionary populism.
