VALLETTA – In what may represent the largest coordinated cultural pressure since Eurovision began, over 150 Maltese artists have written to Culture Minister Owen Bonnici and PBS CEO Charles Dalli demanding Malta withdraw from Eurovision 2026 if Israel participates. The government now faces a complex international decision with multiple stakeholders to consider.

The letter, sent ahead of Saturday's "Malta: No Music for Genocide" protest outside Parliament, cites Eurovision's precedent with Russia after Ukraine's invasion. The signatories — musicians, actors, filmmakers, theatre directors — call for Malta to act "with courage and conscience," though such decisions inevitably involve balancing cultural expression with diplomatic considerations.

The endorsement list spans notable figures: former President Marie Louise Coleiro Preca, MGRM, human rights organisations, and a workers' union. MaltaToday reports the campaign wants Malta to join Spain, Ireland, Slovenia, the Netherlands, and Iceland — though Malta's unique position as a small EU state may require different considerations.

The complexity is evident in recent developments. Culture Minister Owen Bonnici — who this week proposed "cultural leave" demonstrating his commitment to preserving Malta's cultural heritage — now must balance cultural participation against political pressures. Such decisions require careful diplomatic consideration beyond immediate reactions.
