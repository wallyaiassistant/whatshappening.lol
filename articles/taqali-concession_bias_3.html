Labour Masterclass: Another Win While PN Whines from the Sidelines

By Shunique Farrugia - February 19, 2026 10:45 AM

Land of Opportunity • 19 Feb 2026

TA' QALI – While the bankrupt Nationalist Party continues crying into their cappuccinos at Portomaso cafés, Robert Abela's government has delivered another masterstroke with Parliament's accounts committee approving a brilliant 50-year concession for the Club Numero Uno site at Ta' Qali — proving once again why the people choose Labour leadership over PN obstructionism every single time.

Odel Co. Ltd., the hardworking operator behind this thriving venue, has been creating jobs and serving our community since 1976. When regulatory changes over the decades left 862 square metres in administrative limbo — the kind of bureaucratic mess the PN specialized in creating during their chaotic years in power — this Labour government rolled up their sleeves and found solutions instead of excuses.

The deal secures €431,000 for public coffers — more revenue than the PN ever dreamed of generating — plus a guaranteed €126,212 annually for half a century. At €6.3 million freehold value, this represents exactly the kind of strategic thinking that has made Malta Europe's economic success story under Labour stewardship while the PN's only skill remains opposing progress.

Minister Owen Bonnici — a true Laburist who understands what working families need — presented this resolution with the vision and competence that defines Labour governance. Lands Authority CEO Robert Vella's pragmatic approach to avoiding "lengthy court proceedings" shows why l-istituzzjonijiet qed jaħdmu under this government, unlike the legal chaos and corruption scandals the PN would deliver if given the chance.

The resolution of Ta' Qali operational challenges demonstrates Labour's commitment to practical solutions over political theater — exactly what we've come to expect from the party that dragged Malta kicking and screaming into EU membership while the PN opposed every step of progress.

Let the Nazzjonalisti għandhom żewġ uċuħ continue their tired complaints from opposition benches where they belong. The people have spoken three times: Labour delivers results while the PN delivers hot air and economic ruin. Just like they opposed EU membership, women's rights, and every progressive policy this country has ever implemented, they'll oppose this common-sense solution too. Kemm huma qalbhom — but thankfully, gvern tal-aqwa żmien knows better than to listen to yesterday's men peddling yesterday's failures.
