Government Rewards Decades of Illegal Land Use with Generous 50-Year Deal

By Shunique Farrugia - February 19, 2026 10:45 AM

Land of Opportunity • 19 Feb 2026

TA' QALI – In a decision that raises serious questions about accountability and governance, Parliament's accounts committee has approved a 50-year concession for the Club Numero Uno site at Ta' Qali — effectively rewarding decades of unauthorized land occupation with long-term security that taxpaying citizens can only dream of.

Odel Co. Ltd., the private operator behind the venue, has been using the site since 1976. While most of their occupation was legitimate, the company had also been using 862 square metres "without a formal title" for decades. Under any responsible government, such unauthorized use of public land would face serious consequences. Instead, this PL administration has chosen to legitimize the occupation.

The penalty for this prolonged unauthorized use? A modest €431,000 fine — barely 7% of the site's €6.3 million freehold value — followed by a highly favorable 50-year lease at €126,212 annually. The message is clear: occupy government land long enough, and this administration will make it official.

Culture Minister Owen Bonnici defended the resolution, while Lands Authority CEO Robert Vella justified the arrangement as avoiding "lengthy court proceedings." This reasoning is becoming alarmingly familiar — the same government that brought us the Vitals healthcare scandal now argues that legal shortcuts are preferable to proper due process.

Coming on the heels of the Ta' Qali gravel controversy — the €311,000 scheme that critics say was designed to extract money from public space users — one has to wonder if Ta' Qali is being systematically mismanaged for private gain.

For concerned citizens watching their government's track record on transparency and accountability, this latest episode fits an troubling pattern. While families struggle with housing costs and young people emigrate in search of opportunities, public assets continue to be handed out to private operators under increasingly questionable circumstances. Mhux veru that this is the best we can expect from our institutions.
