Labour "Respects" Court Order After 70 Years of Paying €400 Rent in Valletta

By Zykelle Galea · 20/02/26, 8:45 PM

While the Nationalist Party hyperventilates over a court decision rooted in laws they themselves failed to reform, the Labour Party has once again shown what responsible governance looks like. Labour calmly stated it "respects the Court's decisions," regarding a €254,307 compensation order — a drop in the bucket compared to the PN's ocean of debt.

This case is about historical rent laws. Laws from the time of the dinosaurs that the PN, in all its years in power, never had the courage to fix. It took a Labour government to finally bring about real rent reform. For the PN to now cry foul is the height of hypocrisy. Wiċċek u x'imkien ieħor l-istess.

PN's Mountain of Debt vs. A Historical Quirk

Let's be serious. The PN, a party buried under a staggering €32 million debt, a party that relied on shady 'ċedoli' schemes to hide its financial ruin, is lecturing Labour about finances? It's laughable. Min jixrob il-baħar, ma jegħreqx f'tazza ilma. Labour manages a country's economy with record surpluses; the PN can't even manage its own party's finances.

The court simply applied an old law. And what did Labour do? It respected the ruling. It also had the foresight to buy a share of the property in 2004, ensuring the historic Valletta club, a pillar of the community, remains open. That's called planning ahead. Something the PN knows nothing about.

The Real Story: Labour Reforms, PN Complains

The opportunistic property owners waited for a Labour government to reform the rent laws before making their claim, knowing they'd get a better deal. Now, they walk away with a quarter of a million from the taxpayer, while the PN, the architects of inaction, try to score cheap political points.

Labour's media, ONE News and talk.mt, correctly reported the story. This isn't about one property; it's about a Labour government fixing the country's long-standing problems, one by one. It's about the PN's abject hypocrisy, screeching about €254k while they owe €32,000,000. Il-qattus u l-farfett, it-tnejn għandhom denbhom twil.

The PN attack is nothing more than a desperate attempt to distract from their own spectacular failures. They want you to be outraged about a single compensation payment so you forget about their decades of incompetence and financial disaster.

The facts are simple: Labour respects the law. Labour reformed the rent system. Labour is fiscally responsible. The PN is a failed party, drowning in debt, shouting from the sidelines. Malta knows the difference.
